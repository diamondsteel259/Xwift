# How Xwift Works: Privacy Technology Explained Simply

Ever wondered how Xwift keeps your transactions completely private? Let's break it down in simple terms.

## The Privacy Stack

Xwift uses four layers of privacy technology:

### 1. Ring Signatures - Hide the Sender

**The Problem:** In Bitcoin, when you send a transaction, everyone can see exactly which coins you spent.

**The Solution:** Ring signatures mix your transaction with 15 others.

**Simple analogy:** Imagine 16 people in a room, and one of them signs a document. You know one of the 16 signed it, but you can't tell which one. That's a ring signature.

**Technical:** Xwift uses CLSAG (Concise Linkable Spontaneous Anonymous Group signatures) - the latest and most efficient ring signature algorithm.

### 2. Stealth Addresses - Hide the Receiver

**The Problem:** In Bitcoin, when someone sends you money, they use your public address. Anyone can see all transactions to that address.

**The Solution:** Stealth addresses create a unique, one-time address for every transaction.

**Simple analogy:** Instead of having one mailbox that everyone sends mail to, every sender gets a unique mailbox address. You're the only one who knows all those mailboxes belong to you.

**Technical:** Uses Diffie-Hellman key exchange to generate one-time public keys.

### 3. RingCT - Hide the Amount

**The Problem:** In Bitcoin, transaction amounts are visible to everyone.

**The Solution:** RingCT (Ring Confidential Transactions) encrypts the amount using cryptographic commitments.

**Simple analogy:** Like putting money in a sealed envelope. The sender knows they put $100 in. The receiver knows they got $100 out. But everyone else just sees a sealed envelope.

**Technical:** Uses Pedersen commitments with Bulletproof+ range proofs (66% smaller than original Bulletproofs).

### 4. Dandelion++ - Hide Your IP

**The Problem:** When you broadcast a transaction, your IP address can be linked to it.

**The Solution:** Dandelion++ obscures transaction origin through two-phase propagation.

**Simple analogy:** Instead of shouting your transaction from a specific location, it's whispered through a chain of people before being announced publicly from a random location.

**Technical:** "Stem phase" propagates to random peer, then "fluff phase" broadcasts widely.

## Putting It All Together

When you send XFT:
1. Your transaction joins a ring of 15 decoys (ring signature)
2. A unique one-time address is created for the receiver (stealth address)
3. The amount is encrypted (RingCT with Bulletproof+)
4. Your IP is obscured during broadcast (Dandelion++)

**Result:** No one can determine:
- âŒ Who sent the transaction
- âŒ Who received the transaction
- âŒ How much was sent

**Answer:** Zero-knowledge proofs.

The transaction proves mathematically that:
âœ… The sender owns the coins being spent
âœ… The amount being spent is valid (no negatives or overflows)

All without revealing who, what, or how much!

Traditional privacy solutions slow things down. Xwift stays fast by:
